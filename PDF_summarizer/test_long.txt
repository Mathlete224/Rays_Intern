## Flying high on Taiwan's wind energy boom

SinoPac View: Century Group is a major participant in Taiwan's wind power development. Final assembly for the Hai Long offshore wind project began in NOV 2024; raw material production for the Phase 1 Fengmiao project kicked off in JAN 2025; and initial production for the Formosa 4 and Wei Lan Hai Changhua projects are slated for 2H25. The Group's capacity expansions in 2025 will help it seize new wind power opportunities in Taiwan and beyond to fuel its sales and earnings growth.

Our 2024/2025 EPS estimates: NT$7.82/12.74 for parent Century Iron and Steel Industrial Co. (9958 TT, CIAS); NT$10.70/18.20 for Century Wind Power Co. (2072 TT, CWP); NT$0.27/8.02 for Century Huaxin Wind Energy Co. (7752 TT, CHWE).

Operations overview and analysis:

## CHWE targets wind turbine tower certification this year

CHWE has engaged in the development of wind turbine tower related technologies since its establishment. In 2021, it inked a technical cooperation agreement with major Danish wind power manufacturer Welcon to adopt EU industrial standards. CHWE currently produces towers with minimum/maximum diameters of 8m/12m, outperforming its domestic peers, which offer ~7.5m products. In JUL 2024, CHWE began the wind turbine generator (WTG) certification process for wind turbine developers. With trial production slated for APR 2025, CHWE aims to pass the certification by end-2025.

## Ample catalysts and capacity for rekindled growth in 2025

Century Group's 2024 sales were mainly generated from the Taipower and Hai Long projects respectively. Key sales contributors in 2025 will comprise Hai Long project's final assembly (completion slated for 2Q25), Phase 1 Fengmiao (final assembly slated for 3Q25), Formosa 4, and Wei Lan Hai Changhua projects.

Eyeing the sharply higher tonnage of jacket foundations for Phase 1 wind farms, e.g., the jacket tonnage for Fengmiao averages 2,200 metric tons (MTs) and can reach 2500-2600 MTs, we anticipate conspicuous sales/earnings growth for 2025. As for the pin pile business, with the completion of CHWE's plant, we expect an annual out of 150-200 pin pile sets, which shall bring NT$4-6bn of sales.

**Taiwan's 3 Rounds of Wind Energy Development**
Taiwan is implementing three rounds of wind energy development programs: Demonstration Incentive Program (DIP), Zones Application for Planning (ZAP), and Zonal Development.
